Campus Resources

There are a wide variety of resources available, many free of charge, and offered right here on our campus. We will introduce the ones we think you might find most interesting now or at some point on your journey to graduation.

Academic Writing Help Centre (AWHC)

The AWHC is a peer help centre where students can go at any step of the writing process to learn how to become more efficient and autonomous writers. If you like helping others and have excellent writing skills, you could become a writing advisor. To apply for a job at the AWHC, contact one of the coordinators.

Faculty Mentoring Centres

Mentors offer a variety of services to help any student who is looking to improve grades or experiencing academic problems. Mentoring services include 19 mentoring centres, 221 devoted student mentors, over 20 different workshop themes, over 70 study groups and a variety of study skills tips available online. You’ve made the right choice in coming in to see a mentor.

Peer Help Centre

The Peer Help Centre (PHC) operates as a peer support service and resource centre. The mandate is to provide the student community with academic, personal, and social support through resource distribution and short-term peer consultation. Services include: tutor referrals, active listening sessions that occur in person or via the Peer Support Phone Line/Chat service, the Mentoring for Youth Program, peer editing, and presentation critique. "We pride ourselves in being a service run by students for students" - Peer Help Centre.

Counselling Services

The Counselling Services Office offers a range of accessible resources and services based on the Stepped Care Model. The student-centered approach aims to support and empower students so that they can thrive and find their own path to academic success and wellness.

Centre for Psychological Services and Research (CPSR)

The CPSR is a training unit of the School of Psychology. They provide psychological services in both English and French to students in the Ottawa-Gatineau region. Services such as Psychological treatment, adult assessment and career counseling may be provided.

University of Ottawa Health Services (UOHS)

The UOHS is a doctor’s office with a team of professionals who offer services to students and employees such as: Walk-in clinics, Travel Medicine, Allergy Injections, Dietitian, Immunization, Immigration Examination, Mental Health, Sexual Health.

Human Rights Office (HRO)

The HRO provide expert advice on policies and practices related to Diversity & inclusion, Accessibility, Employment equity, and Prevention of harassment, discrimination and sexual violence.

Student Rights Office

Offering guidance, support and information about University of Ottawa regulations and practices to Undergrad and Graduate Students. Students may communicate with the SRC in relation to their Human Right on campus, to review university decisions and get help to file a formal or informal complaint

Financial Aid Office

The Financial Aid and Awards office encourages University of Ottawa students to fulfill their dream of pursuing post-secondary education. They provide students with access to a variety of financial resources, tools and information; reward academic excellence with awards; offer valuable expertise on government loans and create job opportunities for students through the Work-Study program.
