True or False? Colleges and universities provide services to students with disabilities under different laws than those that govern services in the elementary and secondary education system.

Reveal answer!
True!

In Ontario, the special education services offered in public schools (Kindergarden to grade 12) are governed by laws falling under which Act?

Reveal answer!
The Education Act (1990)

The Education Act, 1990 in Ontario allows for the accommodation and support of identified students without a formal disability diagnosis. Some identified students are granted an individual education plan (IEP). It should be noted that identification as an exceptional student or being granted an IEP in high school is not the same as a diagnosis of a permanent disability. At the University of Ottawa, only students with verified disabilities are entitled to academic accommodations with proper documentation. A prior history of accommodation in high school on its own does not warrant the provision of similar accommodations at the University of Ottawa.

Once you get to postsecondary education, the Education Act still drives the accommodation services offered in University

Reveal answer!
False!

The University of Ottawa provides reasonable and appropriate accommodations to students with a known disability as required under the Ontario Human Rights Code, not the Education Act. Only students with a known disability are entitled to accommodations at the post-secondary level.
