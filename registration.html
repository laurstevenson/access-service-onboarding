Registering for accommodations

The process for registering for Academic Accommodations at University of Ottawa requires several steps. These include creating a Ventus account, completing an Intake form, submitting documentation and booking an appointment.

Create a Ventus Account

What is Ventus?

You may have heard that our University uses a tool called Ventus. What is it?

  • Ventus is the Accommodation Management application designed to decrease the work required of students with disabilities, to streamline, simplify and speed up communication regarding student accommodations.
  • It is used to facilitate information sharing between all of the partners in the accommodation process.
  • Ventus is an online portal used by students, learning specialists, professors and faculty to facilitate the adapted examination and accommodation process.
The Ventus student portal lets you create your own account, which guarantees that all of your personal information is kept confidential. Ventus will allow you to:
  • submit medical documentation
  • keep track of all your present and past accommodations
  • see your appointments dates and times
  • see your exams throughout the semester
  • confirm exam participation & select exam accommodations
  • get reminders of when your medical documents or accommodations expire
  • keep track of the letters issued by your learning specialist.
  • access to the transcriptions request form if you need to have textbooks and readings transcribed into an alternate format
  • access your profile on the go using either a smartphone or tablet
Where can I find Ventus?

Ventus is found in several places on the uOttawa website:
1) Through the uOzone application: Login to your uOzone account and you will find Ventus in the "Applications" tab.
or
2)Directly on the Ventus website, a quick Google search for "Ventus uOttawa" will bring you there.
Please note: you will have to login using your uOttawa username and password.

Who has access to Ventus?

All documentation added to Ventus is confidential. Only Academic Accommodations staff have access to this information. After submitting the information, our reception is automatically notified and we will contact you to ensure you know the steps to follow!

Your professor and faculty members also have a Ventus Portal but with different access to information. This is where they find out which students registered in their courses also registered for Academic Accommodations. Therefore, faculty will have access to your name and accommodations only.

Complete the Intake form

What is the intake form?

The intake form is a short questionnaire about previous accommodations, your habits, abilities, and strengths, and the tools you already used to be successful in school. After creating an account, you will be asked to fill out the intake form just one time. It lets the Learning Specialist know a little more about your learning skills, in-class and out-of-class before you meet to work together on identifying what your needs and accommodations.

Your answers will give your learning specialist insight about your challenges and supports for your academic journey.

Where do I find the Intake form?

The intake form is available when you first open your Ventus account.

Ensure that you have some time to spare to complete the questionnaire when you open your account. Once you hit submit, or if you skip this step, you will no longer be able to access the intake form.

Do my answers influence my accommodation plan?

The Learning Specialist will review the intake form prior to your meeting.

The intake form is a great way to help us better understand your academic challenges and current needs through your own eyes.

Therefore, all documents and forms are taken into consideration while building your accommodation plan with the learning specialist.

Enter disability related documentation

What kind of documentation do I need?

Students seeking academic accommodations must provide us with medical documentation completed by a health care practitioner familiar with their disability who is licensed to make a diagnosis. This documentation could include:

  • Medical Certificate
  • Mental Health Medical Certificate
  • Psycho-educational assessments completed within the last five years or at the age of 18
  • Neuropsychological assessments completed within the last five years or at the age of 19
  • Vision assessments
  • Audiology assessments
What is considered proper documentation?

You should carefully review the documentation requirements on our website for each disability category and the health care practitioners qualified to complete the documentation. The type of documentation required depends on the nature of your disability. We know…It can get confusing!

Disability documentation should:

  • Verify that you are a person with a disability. Disability being a protected ground in the Ontario Human Rights Code, only students with disabilities are entitled to academic accommodations at the University of Ottawa
  • Include your name, signature, date of birth
  • Your physician or health practitioner's name, contact information, and license registration number
  • Provide our office with sufficient information about your disability
  • Clearly describe the functional limitations that you experience as a result of your disability and how they impact your access to the learning environment
  • Whether the disability is considered permanent or temporary

Individual Education Plan (IEP): An IEP is a document issued by your high school listing approved academic accommodations. It confirms only that you were identified as a student requiring supports. Students without disabiliies can also benefit from an IEP, as such, an IEP is not considered medical documentation for academic accommodations at the post-secondary level. Interim academic accommodations may be granted for one term.

Who can fill out medical documentation?

Documentation must be filled out and signed by a licensed, certified, practicing health professional who is familiar with your disability and qualified to make the diagnosis.

Important Information on Learning Disorders & psycho-educational assessments!

In Ontario, a Learning Disorder can only be formally diagnosed through a comprehensive psycho-educational assessment completed by a registered clinical psychologist, clinical psychological associate or neuropsychologist.
Assessments and documentation by Speech-Language Pathologists, Occupational Therapists and family physicians are not considered appropriate medical documentation for a Learning Disorder.

What if I cannot get any documentation right now?

We advise that you book an appointment with your physician or health practionnier as soon as possible.

If you have outdated documentation (i.e. greater than 3+ years), medical notes which confirm a diagnosis or certain limitations, an IEP or PEA, this could help to start the accommodation process while you acquire additional medical documentation.

If my medical conditions or learning difficulties are undetermined:

If your health professional is still investigating your diagnosis, our office may consider temporary supports and accommodations with a medical note from your treating family physician or medical specialist.

If you do not have documentation or a confirmed diagnosis, but believe to have a learning difficulty, please contact our service for further information. We can meet with you to discuss your options.

How do I provide my documentation?

Once you have your medical documentation, there are a few different ways to share it with our office. Here are a few options:

  1. Directly on Ventus: Go on your Ventus Portal and open the tab called “Profile”. You will find a button, “+ Add disability information”. You will be able to add the file directly on Ventus this way.
  2. Your doctor may fax the document(s) to the following number: 613-562-5159.
  3. By email to adapt@uottawa.ca
  4. Unfortunately, at this time, you cannot bring your documentation directly to our office. In person services are not available until further notice.

Book an Initial Appointment with a Learning Specialist

How do I book an appointment?

Once you follow all the previous steps, you can book an initial meeting with a Learning Specialist by contacting us:

  • Sending an email to adapt@uottawa.ca
  • By telephone at 613-878-0146
  • In person in the Desmarais Building, room 3172

What should I expect during my initial appointment?

Your initial appointment will last approximately 1hr. During that hour, the learning specialist (LS) will discuss with you about the disability-related academic challenges, needs, and strengths. The LS will ask you some questions regarding your previous experience in the classroom and during evaluations. With the help of the medical documentation and your conversation, you will build an accommodation plan together.

Once set up, you will find the details of your accommodation plan in your Ventus profile. Once you have enrolled in a course, you need to select which accommodations you will need for each course. Your professors need to notify our Exam Centre about the exams in classes. When professors submit their notice of examination (NOE), you will immediately receive an email requesting that you to select your accommodations and confirm your participation to write this exam.


Can I follow-up with my Learning Specialist?

Of course! Your learning specialist can help you throughout your academic journey. They can offer several different strategies for various topics such as Exam Review, Time Management Skills, Stress Management, Note taking Skills… and more! Ask your LS about working on strategies together. They might also refer you to other services that specialize in certain areas, such as the Academic Writing Help Centre or the Assistive Technology Lab
Follow up metings with Learning Specialists can involve:

  • Discussing Learning Strategies such as time management skills, organization or managing stress during exams.​
  • Reviewing accommodations if new documentation is provided by the student.​
  • Developing communication strategies and self-advocacy skills for interacting with professors and faculty.​
  • Discuss technology that may support the student on a case-by-case basis.
