What are some of the challenges that students with temporary or permanent disabilities may face in the classroom and/or during evaluations?

Reveal answer!
  • Accessible barriers: classrooms not being wheelchair accessible or too far apart, for example.
  • Physical limitations: Unable to write an exam due to a broken arm or the need to use assistive technology not offered in the classroom
  • Medical limitations: Fatigue, appointments with medical specialists, fluctuating condition
  • Learning difficulties: Information processing, reading comprehension, written expression

There are many more examples of challenges that students with temporary or permanent disabilities may face, these limitations are unique to you.

How can Academic Accommodations provide help to students with disabilities?

Reveal answer!

Our office supports students by establishing classroom and exam accommodations, being mindful that accommodations do not guarantee academic achievement; students with accommodations will experience similar challenges and successes as their peers do in their programs. Additionally, we can develop a learning strategy plan and help you navigate the different resources on campus.
