Disability definitions and types

What is a disability?

  • Defining disability is a complex, evolving matter. The term “disability” covers a broad range and degree of conditions, which create impairments, activity limitations and participation restrictions.
  • A disability may have been present at birth, caused by an accident, or developed over time. Disability is the interaction between a health condition (e.g. cerebral palsy, or depression) and personal and environmental factors (e.g. inaccessible public buildings or teaching formats).
  • “Disability can be situational in nature, dependent on environmental factors and circumstances.” (NEAD, 2018)
  • Therefore, a person with a disability does not automatically mean that they are disabled.
